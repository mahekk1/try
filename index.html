The group, Harry Pawter and the Chamber of Paws, is here to promote and spread awareness on the extremely concerning topic, which is still not known to a large population, wildlife conservation.
Wildlife conservation is the practice of protecting wild species and their habitats in order to maintain healthy populations of wildlife and to restore, protect, or enhance natural ecosystems.
It is a complex and challenging issue, but it is essential for the future of our planet. By working together, we can protect wildlife and ensure that future generations can enjoy the benefits of a healthy and diverse natural world.

Wildlife conservation is essential for:

• Maintaining the delicate balance of ecosystems
• Preserving biodiversity
• Supporting human well-being
• Ensuring a sustainable future for all life on Earth.
• Ensure the survival of wild species and their habitats
• Providing benefits to humans, such as clean air and water, pollination, and pest control
• Regulating the climate -
• Sustaining our cultures

It is a shared responsibility that involves governments, organizations, communities, and individuals working together to protect and sustain the natural world.

About Us

Meet the next generation of wildlife conservationists! These young people are passionate about protecting animals and their habitats. They are working hard to make a difference in the world, and they are making a real impact.

Hello everyone and welcome to our website. We are the 8th Avanti group from 8th grade Hiranandani Foundation School, and are performing our level best to spread awareness on wildlife conservation! We are dedicated to protecting and preserving the natural world.

Our main goals:
• Try our best to conserve wildlife: This includes ways reducing usage of plastic, reducing pollution, recyclining, etc.
• Educate public regarding the topic as it is of great concern: This includes raising awareness of the threats to wildlife, teaching people about sustainable practices, and encouraging people to get involved in conservation efforts..
• Divide our plan: We intend to divide our plan of action into 2 halves - 1 for pets and 1 for wildlife
• Make an outreach program: We want to make an outreach program by informing people about different eco-systems.
